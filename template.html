This line chart is about balance of payment of import and export respectively. As far as we can see, those two lines show a highly similar pattern in terms of values over those years. The whole pattern shows a increasing trend other than 2001, 2008 and 2020. Obviously, the financial crisis in 2008 caused this significant drop. In addition, the covid outbreak in 2020 also exerted similar effects on international trades due to economy downturn caused the lockdown orders issued by governments. Also, since 2000, the money flow of import is always larger than it of export.

This chart illustrates status of total balance of payment. As far as can be seen from the chart, the value has been negative since 2000. This chart shows the summary result of combined data from first line chart. Since 2000, the U.S. has been suffering international trade deficits.

Detail

This graph shows ten end-use categories in terms of import activities. The covid has impacted the market significantly. Due to the economic downturn, a vast number of business activities have been suppressed. Consequently, the logistic industry will be a major victim (reference: https://www.ttnews.com/articles/pandemic-exacts-heavy-financial-toll-trucking-companies). As far as we can see from this chart, all automobile and petroleum related items are declining, such as "Passenger cars, new and used," "Crude oil," "Other parts and accessories of vehicles" and "Petroleum products" show a significant decline. On the contrary, because of remote education and work from home order, the U.S. market shows a surge at computer need (Reference: https://www.wsj.com/articles/pc-demand-during-pandemic-drives-strongest-u-s-market-growth-in-a-decade-11602537956). As a result, "Computer" and "Computer Accessories" are dramatically increasing.

This graph sketches statistic information about ten end-use categories in terms of export activities. Due to the global recession, downturn in logistic industry was not a sole issue in U.S. Based on other studies, the logistic industry was experiencing constant drop in 2020 (Reference: https://www.sdcexec.com/risk-compliance/press-release/21117361/agility-logistics-industry-expects-global-recession-in-2020), which affected trading amount of certain products, such as "Civilian aircraft, engines, equipment, and parts," "Petroleum products, other", "Fuel oil," "Other parts and accessories of vehicles" and "Passenger cars, new and used" in a negative way. Trading amount of those mentioned products has shown significant reduction.

This graph divides import products at national level into several end-use categories. Being similar to 2008, the trading amount of those six categories in 2020 shows recession except foods. Specifically, capital goods, consumer goods and other goods show a slight drop. However, industrial supplies and automotive vehicles were dropping dramatically. When couple with graphic information and external studies, we can reach a conclusion that the global production was affected significantly and the general trend had hit a historical low point. According to Reuters, U.S. manufacturing activity plunges to 11-year low as orders sink (Reference: https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-economy/u-s-manufacturing-activity-plunges-to-11-year-low-as-orders-sink-idUSKBN22D5M0) and the graphic information has corroborate this conclusion.

This line graph is illustrating trading amount of those several end-use categories in export activities. We can tell from the chart, the export activities show the similar with pattern import activities. Other than foods category, the other five categories were declining simultaneously. Capital goods and industrial supplies were plunging. I would like to conclude that automobile, capital goods and industrial supplies can be seen as a whole. Due to the lockdown order, high unemployment rate and economic recession, consumption level was remaining at a low point, which is a major driven-force in low production subsequently. Meanwhile, the needs toward capital goods and logistic services are suppressed as well.
